Understanding Gender-Based Violence

GBV can take many forms. Recognition is the first step to getting help.

Physical Violence

Hitting, slapping, pushing, kicking, choking, or any act that causes physical harm or pain.

Sexual Violence

Rape, assault, harassment, forced sexual acts, or any unwanted sexual contact without consent.

Emotional Abuse

Threats, intimidation, humiliation, isolation, constant criticism, or controlling behavior.

Economic Abuse

Controlling money, preventing work, denying access to finances, or creating financial dependence.

Stalking & Harassment

Unwanted following, monitoring, repeated calls/messages, showing up uninvited, or online harassment.

Other Forms

Female genital mutilation, forced marriage, human trafficking, or harmful traditional practices.

Why Reporting Matters

Breaking the silence saves lives—including yours

Protection & Safety

Reporting helps create a safety plan and connect you with protection services, shelters, and legal support.

Medical Care

Access immediate medical attention, treatment for injuries, and documentation for potential legal action.

Counseling Support

Professional counseling helps process trauma, rebuild confidence, and develop coping strategies.

Justice & Accountability

Reporting creates a record, holds perpetrators accountable, and can prevent future violence.
